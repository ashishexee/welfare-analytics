# Submission Guidelines

## Program Timeline & Execution Stages

| Stage | Name | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1** | Registration & Orientation | Team formation, track selection, initial competency mapping. Introductory workshops on Design Thinking for Social Impact. |
| **Stage 2** | Problem Release & Letter of Intent | Detailed problem statements released per track. One-week window to finalise chosen problem and submit LoI outlining proposed approach. |
| **Stage 3** | Mentorship & Development Phase | Core developmental phase. Teams paired with track-specific mentors (Faculty, Industry, NGO, Alumni). Primary and secondary research. Initial conceptual frameworks and low-fidelity prototypes. |
| **Stage 4** | Mid-Term Review | Teams present progress, data findings, and initial prototypes to evaluation panel. Constructive feedback to pivot, refine, or accelerate direction. |
| **Stage 5** | Final Submission | Submission of complete Problem Analysis Report, finalised Solution Framework, and functional Prototype or Implementation Plan. Technical documentation and impact projections locked in. |
| **Stage 6** | Showcase & Awards (Grand Finale) | Top teams pitch solutions to a panel of industry leaders, NGO directors, and university faculty. Winners announced; incubation pathways initiated for viable prototypes. |

## Evaluation Criteria & Rubric

| Criterion | Weight | What Evaluators Look For |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem Understanding** | **20%** | Depth of root-cause analysis; quality of stakeholder research; evidence of field-level understanding. |
| **Innovation & Creativity** | **20%** | Originality of approach; novel application of existing tools; creative reframing of the problem. |
| **Feasibility** | **20%** | Technical, financial, and operational viability within realistic constraints. |
| **Impact Potential** | **25%** ← **highest weight** | Projected scale, depth, and sustainability of societal benefit. **Mathematically sound impact projections weighted highly.** |
| **Presentation & Communication** | **15%** | Clarity, professionalism, and persuasiveness of final pitch and written deliverables. |

### Scoring Anchors

| Criterion | Strong Score | Weak Score |
|---|---|---|
| Problem Understanding | Data-backed problem framing; primary research with community stakeholders; clear articulation of who is affected and why current solutions fail | Surface-level description; reliance on Wikipedia-style definitions; no evidence of user research |
| Innovation & Creativity | Unique solution angle not addressed by existing products; creative use of technology or business models; cross-disciplinary synthesis | Replication of existing solutions without meaningful differentiation; incremental thinking |
| Feasibility | Clear resource plan; realistic timeline; identified risks with mitigations; technology stack is appropriate and achievable | Overly ambitious scope with no risk plan; dependence on unproven or inaccessible technology |
| Impact Potential | **Quantified beneficiary reach; cost-per-impact calculation; sustainability plan; pathway to NGO or government adoption** | Vague statements like "will help many people"; no numerical projections; no plan for sustainability |
| Presentation & Communication | Structured narrative; compelling data visualisation; confident delivery; professional documentation | Cluttered slides; inability to answer panel questions; jargon-heavy without substance |

> **Note:** Impact Potential (25%) carries the highest single weight. Teams **must** include mathematically sound projections of lives improved or capital saved.

## Mentorship Model

| Mentor Type | Expertise |
|---|---|
| **Faculty Mentors** | Academic rigour and theoretical validation. Research methodologies, domain knowledge, literature review. |
| **Industry Experts** | Scalability assessment and technical validation. Enterprise-grade architecture, technology best practices, market sizing. |
| **NGO Partners** | Ground-level reality checks and community access. Field operations, cultural nuances, last-mile delivery, beneficiary insights. |
| **Alumni Guidance** | Project management and motivational support. Program navigation, networking, presentation skills. |

## Incentives & Pathways to Incubation

### Certificates of Excellence
- **Provided to:** All top-performing teams at the Grand Finale.
- **Eligibility:** All teams that complete the full program lifecycle.
- **Pathway:** Awarded at the Showcase event; added to official NSS records.

### Exclusive Industry Mentorship
- **Provided to:** Post-event 1-on-1 mentorship with senior industry leaders for finalists.
- **Eligibility:** Top-performing teams selected by the evaluation panel.
- **Pathway:** Facilitated introductions via NSS IIT Roorkee alumni network.

### Incubation Support & Seed Grants
- **Provided to:** Direct incubation support and seed grant pathways for viable prototypes.
- **Eligibility:** Top-ranked prototypes with demonstrated scalability and impact.
- **Pathway:** Referral to IIT Roorkee's entrepreneurship cell and partner incubators.

### Social Impact Showcase
- **Provided to:** Public exhibition attended by CSR heads, venture philanthropists, and media.
- **Eligibility:** Top solutions selected by the evaluation panel.
- **Pathway:** Featured at the Grand Finale showcase event.

## Submission Guidelines

- **Accepted Links:** A Google Drive link or a GitHub repository containing all deliverables.
- **Team Submissions:** Every team member must individually submit the project, even if sharing the same link.
- **Accessibility:** Link must be publicly accessible with appropriate sharing permissions throughout the evaluation period.
- **Submission Limits:** Maximum **two** project submissions per student will be verified and evaluated. Focus on quality.

### Submission Portal Requirements

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Name of Participant | Your full name as enrolled |
| Department & Year | Your academic department and current year of study |
| Problem Statement Chosen | The exact track and problem number selected |
| Submission Link | Google Drive folder or GitHub repository URL |
